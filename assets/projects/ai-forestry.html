Overview

The southeastern US forestry industry plants 1.5 billion seedlings annually, generating over $300 billion in economic activity. However, climate volatility has increased first-year seedling mortality by 15-20%, resulting in $500 million annual losses. Current inventory methods rely on labor-intensive manual counting and visual health assessments that detect problems only after symptoms appearâ€”too late for effective intervention.

Innovation & Technology ResilientSeedling develops an integrated AI-powered platform combining:

  • Hyperspectral UAV sensing capturing hundreds of spectral bands vs. traditional 4-5 band multispectral imaging.
  • Laboratory-validated stress signature library identifying unique spectral responses for drought and freeze damage 8-10 days before visual symptoms.
  • Deep learning algorithms achieving >90% accuracy in preliminary testing for stress detection and classification.
  • Automated seedling inventory replacing manual counting with computer vision-based enumeration.
Our breakthrough approach creates controlled laboratory stress conditions to develop reference spectral signatures, then uses pattern-matching AI to detect these signatures in field conditions via drone-mounted hyperspectral cameras.
